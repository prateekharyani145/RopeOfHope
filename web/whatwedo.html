What we Do

At Rope of Hope, we focus on building the firm basic foundation in functional literacy in lower India. The current Indian education system faces various problems in the classroom, with our heads filled with whys and lectures emptied from learning.

Our goal is to teach children how to learn instead of feeding information to them. We aim to bring zeal for learning in students with the help of unique approaches focusing on the applicability of the subject. We hope forour children to explore their dormant potential and generate abstract thinking.

The direction in which education starts a man will determine his future life.

Project Dream School

It is an initiative to change classrooms into functional spaces and take teaching methods beyond chalk-and-talk in most under-resourced schools with Eco-friendly and sustainable approaches. Additional emphasis is established on hygiene.

If they do not have a textbook, then let there not be a need of it.

Our program has three main fragments to suit the diverse requirements of the students.
It includes Daily classes at Middle School 28, Creative Saturdays and Remedial Sundays.

Daily Classes at Middle School 28

Individuals,driven by enthusiasm to transform the sketchof a usual classroom into a uniquely designed platform for children to learn traditional curriculum serve as full-time teachers to students of a government school. Volunteers teach History, mathematics, English, Hindi, and science concentrating oninsightful and practical learning.From Monday to Friday the classes follow a regular schedule.

Creative Saturday's

Among the hustle of learning and teaching the foundation subjects, Rope of Hope entitlesstudents to dedicate a day to artful activities such as art and craft, photography, music, dance or outdoor activities inclusive of sports. These Saturdays work as mind boosters that ultimately help a child to learn and retain better, it conditions the mind.

Creative Saturdays are also inspired by festivals and holidays that teach them a little more each time about the same. We have made paper lanterns and diyas on Diwali, a sculpture of Lord Ganesha on Ganesha Chaturthi, kites on Makar Sankranti, understood the importance of water on Holi and many more. Students also enjoy sketching, painting, best out of waste and continue to justify the joyous platform.

Remedial Sunday's

Sundays are dedicated to the children of Slums in Bhawarkua, Indore. We provide remedial classes to these students for foundation subjects that are English, Hindi, and basic Mathematics. Compassionate individuals dedicate the time to under-resourced students and provide a sense of direction with basic education.

Mission Smile

A smile is the most necessary thing a child should wear.

Rope Of Hope celebrates Children's Day with hundreds of students. The first mission smile was accomplished on 14th November 2018 with more than a thousand slum students. In 2019 students from 5 official government schools joined Us.

Day the whole nation dedicated to children is most cheerfully celebrated by us. The last mission smile revolved around gladsome activities. Children participated in Drawing competition and Best out of Waste. It was graced by a motivational speaker Pratik Uppal and we were enlightened with his words. To energise the segment treasure hunt was organised. A fun piece of riddles and puzzles glimmered the faces and laughter were captured.

Students show casted their talents with Yoga and Dance. The event winded up with food stalls set up by students. It was a mission well accomplished.
